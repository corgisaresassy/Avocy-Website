The Avocy Difference

Telehealth Therapy

Avocy's unique telehealth therapy program connects patients with insurance covered, evidence based care up to three times a week. Avocy tailors it's outpatient program to the patient needs and helps ensure patients work with one of our expert addiction focused therapists to receive the best possible care in their recovery journey

Multiple Sessions

Avocy offers up to insurance-covered telehealth therapy to people of all ages recovering from substance use disorder and other mental health issues. We understand it can be difficult to connect with a new therapist when you only meet once per week. To overcome this challenge, we recommend and encourage patients to meet with their therapist twice a week for the first month.

At Avocy, we accept most major insurance plans. For most patients, the copay associated with two sessions is still less than the cost of one session with a private therapist. We believe no one should have to choose between living and self-care. Contact us today to learn more about your insurance and how Avocy can help you move forward.

Diverse Clinical Interventions

Our clinical team has experience with a wide range of clinical interventions to find the best approach for you. Work directly with your therapist on the long-term work associated with PTSD, anxiety, managing triggers, and building healthy relationships. We are here to help you receive the possible care in your recovery journey.

Our expert clinical staff have experience with a wide range of evidence-based interventions including: Existentialism, DBT, ACT, EMDR, and CBT. If you don’t know the acronyms, don’t worry. Work with your therapist to understand the best approach that fits your needs.

How Does Therapy Work?

Therapy is about having a safe place for self-reflection, for understanding actions and responses, and for self-exploration. Managing the human condition is a skill. Whether it come to managing intrusive thoughts, fears, grief, or finding purpose and direction, a therapist can help you explore your inner thoughts.

Is Therapy Right For Me?

At some point in life, everyone will benefit from therapy. Relationships, loss, failure, and depression are common occurrences. Navigating complex emotions can be challenging and overwhelming. Therapy isn’t about telling you the answer, but helping you to understand your own emotions and feelings to make more informed decisions.

When Can I Expect Results?

Therapy doesn’t have a finish line. It also doesn’t always feel good to explore underlying trauma and emotions. Therapy is a process of self-discovery and learning. You’ll work with your therapist to discuss your short-term and long-term goals, and our team will support you across every step.
